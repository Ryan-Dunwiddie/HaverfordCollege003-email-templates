You and 8 to 16 other Fords on the floor of your residence hall, along with 4 returning students who serve as your mentors, will form a Customs group. Together, you will navigate the first year at Haverford.

You'll compete in the Dorm Olympics, meet new friends at the Supa Fun Dance, go out for group dinners, and learn about everything from The Honor Code to the resources available for mental health. Above all, you'll learn how to make space for one another's opinions, passions, and interests—all while creating enduring friendships.

“To this day, I still know all the names of the people on my floor from first year,” said Carina Whiting '25, history major. To be a Ford is to be known, heard, and seen.

Customs programming is entirely designed and planned by students: from the creation of Customs groups to the activities you’ll participate in. Programming is revised each year based on student feedback.
