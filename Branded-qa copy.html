One thing you won’t see during final exams: a professor

For 127 years, Haverford College students have maintained The Honor Code: not a set of rules but a statement of shared values around academic and social aspects of campus life centered on concepts of trust, concern, and respect. As a result, professors are not present during final exams, students self-govern in our residence halls without RAs, labs are open 24 hours a day, and students are able to influence every aspect of the student experience.
